Lechon Paksiw

About the dish

This dish is a staple in Filipino households. Whenever there's a fiesta and there is some leftover Lechon?
This is always the go to dish to not waste anything. That is why in Kusina la Sofia's exclusive menu,
this dish is a must have. Every paksiw has the same foundation of flavor, but depending on the ingredients
and how you cook it, they differ in taste. So this dish, tastes different depending on the chef and culture.
It's somewhat a personal dish that is why my wife loves mine so much. It reflects my personality and own
take on the classic that is the Lechon Paksiw.

Ingredients

  • Leftover Lechon (obviously)
  • Garlic
  • Whole Peppercorns
  • Sugar
  • Bay Leaves
  • Soy Sauce
  • Vinegar;preferably Apple Cider vinegar (tuba in the vernacular)
  • Seasoning (Ginisa Mix or Maggie Magic Sarap)

Instructions

  1. Pound the cloves of garlic and peel off skin, do not cut or dice. (Tastes better whole).
  2. Using a skillet, saute the garlic clove until aromatic.
  3. Add the leftover lechon and stir
  4. Add a drizzle of Soy Sauce and Oyster Sauce if you prefer.
  5. Add Peppercorns and Bay Leaves.
  6. Stir and then add a generous amount of Vinegar.
  7. Stir then add Sugar depending on how sweet you want it to taste.
  8. Stir again then add water. (enough to submerge the Lechon).
  9. Put a lid on and cook it for 15-20 minutes to let the soup and the flavor of the lechon to mesh.
  10. Serve with a serving of rice.
